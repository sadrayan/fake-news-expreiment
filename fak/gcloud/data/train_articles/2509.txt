Lawmakers: Military Pay Law Covers Civilians, Contractors
Agence France-Presse/Getty Images

A growing list of lawmakers is urging the Pentagon to embrace a liberal view of a recently signed military pay measure and exempt a large number of military contractors and Defense Department civilians from forced furloughs during the government shutdown.

In a series of letters and statements, Republicans and Democrats have called on Defense Secretary Chuck Hagel to allow tens of thousands of federal workers and contractors to return to their jobs.

Sens. Jeanne Shaheen (D., N.H.) and Kelly Ayotte (R., N.H.), are among those urging Mr. Hagel to use his “maximum discretion” to blunt the impact of the shutdown, which has forced the Defense Department to furlough upwards of 400,000 civilian workers…
