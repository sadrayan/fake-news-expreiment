Mexican American trailblazers recognized by Austin History Center
If you go:

A reception for 'Mexican American Firsts: Trailblazers of Austin and Travis Count'' will be at 3 p.m. today at the Austin History Center, 810 Guadalupe St. The exhibit will run through Jan. 16 at the center.

Other planned related programs include:

• 'Unraveling Our Historical Past: Mexican Americans in Austin and Travis County,' 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Oct. 2 at the Emma Barrientos Mexican American Cultural Center, 600 River St.

• 'Linking the Past to the Future: Latino/A Genealogy Workshop,' 1 to 4 p.m. Nov. 13 at the Austin History Center.

• 'A Conversation With Six Mexican American Trailblazers,'

1 to 3 p.m. Dec. 11 at the history center.

• 'Vamos a Platicar: Preserving Austin's Mexican American History' and a tour of the history center, 1 to 4 p.m. Jan. 8.

• A traveling companion exhibit will begin Sept. 11 at the Mexican American Cultural Center and continue to other locations in Austin and Travis County, including Martin Middle School. Dates for other locations have not been announced.

The exhibit, reception and programs are free. For more information, contact Gloria Espitia at 974-7498 or visit www.cityofaustin.org/library/ahc/trailblazers.html
