House OKs Bill To Cut Meat Labeling Rules After WTO Loss

Law360, Washington (June 10, 2015, 10:05 PM EDT) -- The U.S. House of Representatives on Wednesday passed legislation to repeal contentious country-of-origin labeling requirements for beef, pork and chicken, seeking to avoid $3 billion in retaliatory tariffs after a World Trade Organization ruling that found U.S. COOL requirements to be discriminatory.
