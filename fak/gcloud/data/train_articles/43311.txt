Lumentum slashes earnings, revenue outlook after large customer asks to reduce shipments
Lumentum Holdings Inc. LITE, -3.94% slashed its fiscal second-quarter revenue and earnings outlook after optical equipment maker said it received a request from "one of its largest" customers to "materially reduce shipments" for previously placed orders. The stock was halted for news until 8:30 a.m. Eastern. The company cut his guidance range for adjusted earnings per share to $1.15 to $1.34 from $1.60 to $1.75, compared with the FactSet consensus of $1.67, and for revenue to $335 million to $355 million from $405 million to $430 million, compared with expectations of $420.5 million. In Lumentum's 10-K annual report for the fiscal year ended June 30, the company said its largest customers in terms of percent of revenue were Apple Inc. AAPL, -1.81% at 30%, Huawei at 11% and Ciena Corp. CIEN, -1.49% at 11%. Lumentum's stock, which was inactive prior to the halt, has lost 4.5% over the past three months while the S&P 500 SPX, -1.32% has slipped 1.8%.
