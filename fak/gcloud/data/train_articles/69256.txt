STATE OF RHODE ISLAND

IN GENERAL ASSEMBLY

JANUARY SESSION, A.D. 2013

____________

A N A C T

RELATING TO ELECTIONS

Introduced By: Representatives Gallison, Blazejewski, Handy, Valencia, and Hull

Date Introduced: February 14, 2013

Referred To: House Judiciary

It is enacted by the General Assembly as follows:

1-1 SECTION; 1. Title 17 of the General Laws entitled "ELECTIONS" is hereby amended by

1-2 adding thereto the following chapter:

1-3 CHAPTER 4.2

1-4 AGREEMENT AMONG THE STATES TO ELECT THE PRESIDENT BY NATIONAL

1-5 POPULAR VOTE

1-6 17-4.2-1. Compact enacted. â€“ The interstate compact entitled the "Agreement Among

1-7 the States to Elect the President by National Popular Vote" is hereby enacted into law and entered

1-8 into with all other jurisdictions legally joining therein in the form substantially as follows:

1-9 ARTICLE I.

1-10 MEMBERSHIP.

1-11 Any state of the United States and the District of Columbia may become a member of this

1-12 agreement by enacting this agreement.

1-13 ARTICLE II.

1-14 RIGHT OF THE PEOPLE IN MEMBER STATES TO VOTE FOR PRESIDENT AND

1-15 VICE PRESIDENT.

1-16 Each member state shall conduct a statewide popular election for president and vice

1-17 president of the United States.

1-18 ARTICLE III.

1-19 MANNER OF APPOINTING PRESIDENTIAL ELECTORS IN MEMBER STATES.

2-20 Prior to the time set by law for the meeting and voting by the presidential electors, the

2-21 chief election official of each member state shall determine the number of votes for each

2-22 presidential slate in each state of the United States and in the District of Columbia in which votes

2-23 have been cast in a statewide popular election and shall add such votes together to produce a

2-24 "national popular vote total" for each presidential slate.

2-25 The chief election official of each member state shall designate the presidential slate with

2-26 the largest national popular vote total as the "national popular vote winner."

2-27 The presidential elector certifying official of each member state shall certify the

2-28 appointment in that official's own state of the elector slate nominated in that state in association

2-29 with the national popular vote winner.

2-30 At least six (6) days before the day fixed by law for the meeting and voting by the

2-31 presidential electors, each member state shall make a final determination of the number of

2-32 popular votes cast in the state for each presidential slate and shall communicate an official

2-33 statement of such determination within twenty-four (24) hours to the chief election official of

2-34 each other member state.

2-35 The chief election official of each member state shall treat as conclusive an official

2-36 statement containing the number of popular votes in a state for each presidential slate made by the

2-37 day established by federal law for making a state's final determination conclusive as to the

2-38 counting of electoral votes by Congress.

2-39 In event of a tie for the national popular vote winner, the presidential elector certifying

2-40 official of each member state shall certify the appointment of the elector slate nominated in

2-41 association with the presidential slate receiving the largest number of popular votes within that

2-42 official's own state.

2-43 If, for any reason, the number of presidential electors nominated in a member state in

2-44 association with the national popular vote winner is less than or greater than that state's number of

2-45 electoral votes, the presidential candidate on the presidential slate that has been designated as the

2-46 national popular vote winner shall have the power to nominate the presidential electors for that

2-47 state and that state's presidential elector certifying official shall certify the appointment of such

2-48 nominees.

2-49 The chief election official of each member state shall immediately release to the public

2-50 all vote counts or statements of votes as they are determined or obtained.

2-51 This article shall govern the appointment of presidential electors in each member state in

2-52 any year in which this agreement is, on July 20, in effect in states cumulatively possessing a

2-53 majority of the electoral votes.

3-54 ARTICLE IV.

3-55 OTHER PROVISIONS.

3-56 This agreement shall take effect when states cumulatively possessing a majority of the

3-57 electoral votes have enacted this agreement in substantially the same form and the enactments by

3-58 such states have taken effect in each state.

3-59 Any member state may withdraw from this agreement, except that a withdrawal

3-60 occurring six (6) months or less before the end of a president's term shall not become effective

3-61 until a president or vice president shall have been qualified to serve the next term.

3-62 The chief executive of each member state shall promptly notify the chief executive of all

3-63 other states of when this agreement has been enacted and has taken effect in that official's state,

3-64 when the state has withdrawn from this agreement, and when this agreement takes effect

3-65 generally.

3-66 This agreement shall terminate if the electoral college is abolished.

3-67 If any provision of this agreement is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall not be

3-68 affected.

3-69 ARTICLE V.

3-70 DEFINITIONS.

3-71 For purposes of this agreement:

3-72 (1) "Chief executive" shall mean the governor of a state of the United States or the mayor

3-73 of the District of Columbia;

3-74 (2) "Elector slate" shall mean a slate of candidates who have been nominated in a state

3-75 for the position of presidential elector in association with a presidential slate;

3-76 (3) "Chief election official" shall mean the state official or body that is authorized to

3-77 certify the total number of popular votes for each presidential slate;

3-78 (4) "Presidential elector" shall mean an elector for president and vice president of the

3-79 United States;

3-80 (5) "Presidential elector certifying official" shall mean the state official or body that is

3-81 authorized to certify the appointment of the state's presidential electors;

3-82 (6) "Presidential slate" shall mean a slate of two (2) persons, the first of whom has been

3-83 nominated as a candidate for president of the United States and the second of whom has been

3-84 nominated as a candidate for vice president of the United States, or any legal successors to such

3-85 persons, regardless of whether both names appear on the ballot presented to the voter in a

3-86 particular state;

3-87 (7) "State" shall mean a state of the United States and the District of Columbia; and

4-88 (8) "Statewide popular election" shall mean a general election in which votes are cast for

4-89 presidential slates by individual voters and counted on a statewide basis.

4-90 SECTION; 2. This act shall take effect upon passage.
