Woodrow Wilson Paris Attended Paris Peace Conference. Arrived In the U.S. February 24. January 7–February 14, 1919

Franklin D. Roosevelt Martinique Conferred with U.S. officials. December 8, 1940

Franklin D. Roosevelt Oran (Algeria) Disembarked en route to Cairo. November 20, 1943

Franklin D. Roosevelt Dakar (Senegal) Re-embarked for the U.S. December 9, 1943

Franklin D. Roosevelt Algiers (Algeria) Briefed U.S. Ambassadors to the United Kingdom, France, and Italy on the Yalta Conference. February 18, 1945

Dwight D. Eisenhower Paris Attended meeting of NATO Heads of Government. December 14–19, 1957

Dwight D. Eisenhower Paris Informal meeting with President De Gaulle and Italian Premier Segni. Addressed North Atlantic Council. September 2–4, 1959

Dwight D. Eisenhower Toulon, Paris Conference with President De Gaulle, British Prime Minister Macmillan, and German Chancellor Adenauer. December 18–21, 1959

Dwight D. Eisenhower Paris Conference with President De Gaulle, British Prime Minister Macmillan, and Soviet Premier Khrushchev. May 15–19, 1960

John F. Kennedy Paris State visit; addressed North Atlantic Council and met with President De Gaulle. May 31–June 3, 1961

Richard M. Nixon Paris Met with President De Gaulle. February 28–March 2, 1969

Richard M. Nixon Paris Attended memorial services for former President De Gaulle. November 12, 1970

Richard M. Nixon Paris Attended memorial services for former President Pompidou. Met afterward with interim President Poher, Italian Prime Minister Leone, British Prime Minister Wilson, West German Chancellor Brandt, Danish Prime Minister Hartling, Soviet President Podgorny, and Japanese Prime Minister Tanaka. April 5–7, 1974

Gerald R. Ford Martinique Met with President Giscard d'Estaing. December 14–16, 1974

Jimmy Carter Guadeloupe Met informally with President Giscard d'Estaing, German Chancellor Schmidt, and British Prime Minister Callaghan. January 4–9, 1979

Ronald Reagan Paris State visit; met with President Mitterrand and Prime Minister Mauroy. June 2–7, 1982

Ronald Reagan Versailles Attended Economic Summit Meeting of the Heads of State and Government of Canada, France, the Federal Republic of Germany, Italy, Japan, and the United Kingdom. June 5–6, 1982

Ronald Reagan Normandy Attended commemorative ceremonies of the 40th anniversary of the Allied landing in Normandy. Also present were Prime Minister Trudeau of Canada, Queen Beatrix of the Netherlands, King Olav V of Norway, King Baudouin I of Belgium, President Mitterrand of France, Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom, and Grand Duke Jean of Luxembourg. June 6, 1984

Ronald Reagan Strasbourg Addressed the European Parliament. May 8, 1985

Also attended ceremonies for the Bicentennial of the French Revolution, and met with Ivorian President Houphouet-Boigny. July 13–17, 1989

George H. W. Bush St. Martin Island (French West Indies) Informal meeting with President Mitterrand. December 16, 1989

George H. W. Bush Paris Attended CSCE Summit Meeting and the signing of the Treaty on Conventional Armed Forces in Europe. November 18–21, 1990

George H. W. Bush Martinique, West Indies Discussed the Middle East peace process with President Mitterrand. March 14, 1991

George H. W. Bush Rambouillet Discussed further sanctions against Iraq with President Mitterrand. July 14, 1991

George H. W. Bush Paris Discussed the Bosnian crisis with President Mitterrand. January 3, 1993

William J. Clinton Paris Attended the signing of the Bosnian peace treaty. December 14, 1995

William J. Clinton Lyon, Perouges, Paris Attended the G-7 Economic Summit Meeting and met with Russian Prime Minister Chernomyrdin and UN Secretary-General Boutrous-Ghali. June 27–29, 1996

William J. Clinton Paris Attended the signing of the NATO-Russia Founding Act. May 26–27, 1997

William J. Clinton Paris Discussed peacekeeping in Kosovo with President Chirac and Prime Minister Jospin. June 16–17, 1999

George W. Bush Paris, Colleville, Caen, Arromanches Met with President Chirac and commemorated the 60th anniversary of D-Day. June 5–6, 2004
