INA: ACT 328 - NATURALIZATION THROUGH SERVICE IN THE ARMED FORCES OF THE UNITED STATES
(a) A person who has served honorably at any time in the Armed Forces of the United States for a period or periods aggregating

one year, and who, if separated from such service, was never separated except under honorable conditions, may be naturalized without having resided, continuously immediately preceding the date of filing such person's application, in the United States for at least five years, and in the State or district of the Service in the United States in which the application for naturalization is filed for at least three months, and without having been physically present in the United States for any specified period, if such application is filed while the applicant is still in the service or within six months after the termination of such service.
