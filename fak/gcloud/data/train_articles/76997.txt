Rape Crisis Centers Address the Media on Governor’s Funding Veto
FCASV and rape crisis centers across the state reacted with shock and dismay when the Governor vetoed $1.5 in new funding for rape crisis services on April 17th especially when the need for increased funding is so clear, waiting lists for core services are growing, and programs can’t meet the needs of those whose lives have been devastated by sexual violence. Program hope to meet with the Governor this summer to help him understand the unmet needs of victims. Many national media sources and blogs covered the story including an article in the New Republic.
