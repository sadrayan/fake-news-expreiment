Klobuchar says NFL players have obligation as role models
Sen. Amy Klobuchar said on Sunday the NFL should hold players accountable for their actions, especially on charges of domestic violence or child abuse.

Appearing on CNN's “State of the Union” the Democratic senator from Minnesota discussed the NFL’s reaction to Baltimore Ravens’ player Ray Rice punching his then-girlfriend in the face and knocking her out in an elevator as well as Minnesota Vikings player Adrian Peterson, who is charged with beating his 4-year-old son with a switch until he bled.

“They have this obligation when they are putting out their players as role models to set a different culture,” Klobuchar said, adding that every minute 24 women are beaten.
