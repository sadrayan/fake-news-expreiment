Watchdog to Probe Consumer Bureau’s Data Collection
Congress’s investigative arm will scrutinize a consumer watchdog’s effort to collect data from financial firms on customer transactions.

The inquiry comes amid industry criticism that it’s onerous for companies and intrusive for consumers to provide such data.

The office of Sen. Mike Crapo (R. Idaho) disclosed Thursday the Government Accountability Office’s probe of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. The issue has become a point of contention in recent weeks between the nearly two-year old regulator and Republicans on Capitol Hill…
