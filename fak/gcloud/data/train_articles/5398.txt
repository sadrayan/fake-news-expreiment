How did early mammals chew? - May, 2019

Unless you have a toothache, it's easy to take chewing for granted. We crunch through foods like nuts and fruit without thinking much about the anatomical equipment that makes it happen. But we know from fossils that our earliest mammalian ancestors didn't have a single lower jaw bone like we do today; instead, they had left and right jaw bones that could move independently.
