Bernie Sanders was born in New York City, New York in September 1941. Sanders is a United States Senator representing Vermont. He is an independent and is affiliated with the Democratic and Vermont Progressive parties. He was an organizer for the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee during the Civil Rights Movement and participated in the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom in 1963. Bernie graduated from the University of Chicago in 1964. Sanders was elected Mayor of Burlington, Vermont in 1981 and served until 1989. From 1991 to 2007 he served as a member of the United States House of Representatives from Vermont's At-large district. Sanders became the junior United States Senator from Vermont in 2007. In 2013 he became Chairman of the Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs. During his term in the House of Representatives he was the only independent member for the majority of his service. He was also the longest-serving independent in the history of U.S. Congress. Sanders describes himself as a democratic socialist. In March 2014 Sanders announced that he was "prepared to run" for President of the United States in 2016.
