Granting the consent of Congress to the Vermont-New Hampshire Interstate Public Water Supply Compact. (1996; 104th Congress H.J.Res. 129)
H.J.Res. 129 (104th) was a joint resolution in the United States Congress.

A joint resolution is often used in the same manner as a bill. If passed by both the House and Senate in identical form and signed by the President, it becomes a law. Joint resolutions are also used to propose amendments to the Constitution.

This joint resolution was introduced in the 104th Congress, which met from Jan 4, 1995 to Oct 4, 1996. Legislation not enacted by the end of a Congress is cleared from the books.
