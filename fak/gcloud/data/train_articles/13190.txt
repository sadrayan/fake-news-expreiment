What's On Today
10 P.M. (Sundance) FULL FRONTAL FASHION The Sundance Channel begins its twice-yearly tribute to New York Fashion Week with seven nights of sartorial-theme programming. First is ''The Day Before,'' a new documentary series from the director Lo?Prigent (''Sign?hanel'' and ''Marc Jacobs & Louis Vuitton'') that follows final preparations for runway presentations by four high-profile fashion houses. The series begins with Sonia Rykiel (above, seated at center), a k a the Queen of Knits, as her family-owned company gears up to celebrate its 40th anniversary in Paris. Other episodes focus on Fendi (Thursday), Proenza Schouler (Friday) and Jean Paul Gaultier (Saturday). ''Somebody Told Me About ... Carla Bruni,'' at 11, looks at the model and singer, and features a cameo from her husband, President Nicolas Sarkozy of France.

7 A.M. (ABC) GOOD MORNING AMERICAPresident Obama is interviewed. Darius Rucker and Lee Ann Womack announce the Academy of Country Music Awards nominees.

11 A.M. (ABC) THE VIEW The guests include former Gov. Rod R. Blagojevich of Illinois; his wife, Patti; and Cindy Crawford. Meghan McCain is the guest host.

8 P.M. (ABC, CBS, CNN, Fox Business Network, Fox News Channel, NBC, 13) PRESIDENTIAL ADDRESS TO A JOINT SESSION OF CONGRESS President Obama discusses his health care initiative.

8 P.M. (CW) AMERICA'S NEXT TOP MODEL This 13th edition caters to women 5-foot-7 and under as 32 contestants converge in Los Angeles, where all but 14 will be quickly eliminated. Then the remaining competitors receive makeovers before being asked to pose for ''baby pictures.'' The model Chanel Iman is a guest judge.

8 P.M. (Fox) SO YOU THINK YOU CAN DANCE Season 6 begins with the requisite audition episode.

8 P.M. (21, 49) FAMILIES STAND TOGETHER: FEELING SECURE IN TOUGH TIMES

Al Roker, Deborah Roberts (near right) and Elmo are hosts of this program, which looks at how families, both human and Muppet, cope with an unstable economy. ''Your Life, Your Money,'' at 9 on 13 and 49, offers basic financial advice on banking, credit and investments as well as on budgeting, insurance and self-employment. Donald Faison hosts.

9 P.M. (Fox) GLEE The glee club gives its first performance, which makes the school's cheerleading coach, Sue Sylvester (Jane Lynch), smirk. But the crowd loves it.

9 P.M. (Showtime); 10 P.M. (Showtime 2) INSIDE THE NFL The series kicks off its 33rd edition, with James Brown, Phil Simms, Cris Collinsworth and Warren Sapp offering National Football League news, highlights and analysis.

9 P.M. (TNT) LEVERAGE In the last episode before the series goes on hiatus, Nate (Timothy Hutton) and his team are hired to investigate a lawyer who is scheming to gain control of a client's estate. But then a long-lost daughter appears.

9 P.M. (Discovery) MAN VS. WILD Bear Grylls journeys to the Alaskan wilderness, where he ice-hops onto a glacier, hangs by his fingertips over a waterfall and just avoids getting smashed between an iceberg and his rescue vessel.

9 P.M. (National Geographic) E-MAIL ORDER BRIDE Two American men go in search of wives in Togliatti, Russia.

9 P.M. (History) NOSTRADAMUS EFFECT The prophet Nostradamus predicted the arrival of three anti-Christs who would wreak havoc on mankind. For those who believe the prophecy, clues suggest that Napoleon may have been the first and Hitler the second. But who is the third? This series premiere speculates.

10 P.M. (13) TIME FOR SCHOOL 3 ''Wide Angle'' concludes its 12-year documentary project with a visit to seven classrooms in seven countries, where seven children struggle to get what most Americans take for granted: a basic education.

10 P.M. (A&E) CRISS ANGEL MINDFREAK In this season finale Mr. Angel hides in one of five upright coffins placed along the Las Vegas Strip, and a driver crashes into each, without knowing where the illusionist is lurking.

10 P.M. (Syfy) DESTINATION TRUTH In this Season 3 premiere Jason Hawes and Grant Wilson from ''Ghost Hunters'' scrutinize strange happenings in the Hoia-Baciu forest in Romania that were witnessed by the show's host, Josh Gates. KATHRYN SHATTUCK
