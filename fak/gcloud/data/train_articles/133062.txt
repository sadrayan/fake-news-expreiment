Andrew Saul (nutrition)
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Andrew W. Saul is Editor-in-Chief of the Orthomolecular Medicine News Service and is on the editorial board of the Journal of Orthomolecular Medicine. He has published some 200 peer-reviewed articles, and has written or coauthored twelve books. Four of these were coauthored with Abram Hoffer, M.D., Ph.D. Many of Saul's books have been translated into foreign languages, including Japanese, Chinese, Hindi, Arabic, Spanish, Norwegian, Polish, and Italian. Saul has twice won New York Empire State Fellowships for teaching. He is a board member of the Japanese College of Intravenous Therapy. Psychology Today magazine named him one of seven natural health pioneers. In 2013, Andrew Saul was inducted into the Orthomolecular Medicine Hall of Fame.[1]

He was featured in the documentary Food Matters and he is known for The MegaVitamin Formula course. He resides in Rochester NY, close to his daughter Helen Saul Case, who also appears in That Vitamin Movie.[2][3]
