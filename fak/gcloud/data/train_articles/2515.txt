World Cup: Liberals More Likely to Be Soccer Fans
Every four years, nations across the globe come together and celebrate the one sport that unites us all – soccer or football or futbol – with the World Cup. Except in the U.S., of course.

Thursday marked the beginning of the 2014 World Cup in Brazil, and, going by the numbers, those of a more liberal political persuasion are more likely to be tuned into the big event than their conservative counterparts. Data from Experian Marketing Services shows liberals are more likely to say they’ve watched the World Cup soccer matches than conservatives – and more likely than middle-of-the-road Americans as well.
